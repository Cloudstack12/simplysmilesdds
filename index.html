What Is Dental Hygiene and Why Is It Important?

It is incredibly important to take care of your dental hygiene, but why? Here's everything you need to know about caring for your oral hygiene.

Read Post

Most people associate dental hygiene with having a bright, white smile . As important as straight, beautiful teeth are, a lot more goes into maintaining good oral health.

Scheduling regular cleanings, brushing and flossing at home, and avoiding certain foods are all part of good oral hygiene. Keep reading to learn more about exactly what dental hygiene is and why it's so important for your overall health and well-being.

What Is Dental Hygiene?

According to one Delta Dental survey, 2% of people don't brush their teeth at all and another 29% only brush once a day. Luckily, 55% of people brush the recommended twice a day for 2 minutes.

Regularly brushing and flossing your teeth removes plaque which, if left on too long, will harden into tartar. Only a dental professional can remove tartar. Untreated tartar buildup can cause cavities, tooth decay, and possible tooth loss.

Here are a few more reasons why investing in your dental hygiene is really an investment in your health.

Oral Hygiene and Overall Health Are Connected

Did you know that the health of your mouth directly impacts your overall health and wellbeing? That's because your mouth acts as a gateway to the rest of your body. It's also the first place systemic diseases present themselves.

For example, diabetes can start as a sore, mouth lesion, or gum disease. Patients with gum disease (or periodontal disease) are at greater risk of developing heart disease, diabetes, or stroke.

When your gums are inflamed, it can cause chronic inflammation in other parts of your body as well, including the arteries that lead to your heart. Other health issues caused by poor dental hygiene include:

  • Bacterial pneumonia
  • Cancer
  • Sepsis
  • Complications during pregnancy or low birth weight

Early Detection

Early detection is key when diagnosing and treating any major health issue, including oral cancer. Without regular dental visits, you could develop a serious health risk and not even know it.

The earlier you spot the warning signs, the more likely you are to find a simple and effective treatment. If an issue arises between visits, never hesitate to call and schedule an emergency appointment .

Prevent Tooth Loss

One way to avoid the need for the above-mentioned procedures is by preventing tooth loss in the first place. According to the American College of Prosthodontists (ACP), 178 million Americans are missing at least one tooth and 40 million are missing all of their teeth.

Periodontal disease along with poor dental hygiene is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. When plaque builds up on your teeth and turns into tartar, it can cause severe tooth decay and gum infections.

By practicing good dental hygiene and seeing your dentist regularly, you can prevent plaque buildup. Brushing and flossing at home are the first steps to maintaining good oral health. Scheduling a cleaning with your dentist in Ashburn, VA, will help remove hard-to-reach plaque and tartar along the gum line.

Tips for Maintaining Good Oral Health and Hygiene

Now that you know the importance of maintaining good dental hygiene, here are a few more tips on how you can invest in those pearly whites.

Avoid Sugary Snacks and Drinks

When you consume too much sugar, plaque bacteria in your mouth produce acid which, over time, attacks your tooth enamel. Enamel is the hard surface that covers and protects your teeth. Once it wears away, you can never get it back.

Carbonated and sports drinks, coffee, tea, and sugary desserts and candies can all break down tooth enamel.

Avoid Tobacco

While it's no secret that smoking cigarettes are bad for your overall health, this habit can also cause a long list of oral health issues. Tobacco stains your teeth, giving them a yellowish tint and killing their natural shine. It can also lead to more plaque and tartar buildup.

Your gums are also negatively affected by tobacco use. Smoking causes a lack of oxygen in your bloodstream, making it increasingly difficult for gum infections to heal.

Rinse with Water or Chew Sugarless Gum

Although it may surprise you that a dentist in Ashburn, VA , would recommend chewing gum, doing so between meals increases saliva, thereby reducing plaque acid and tooth decay. Sugarless gum may also help strengthen your teeth and act as a replacement when brushing isn't possible.

You can also rinse with water between meals to remove any food debris stuck between your teeth.
